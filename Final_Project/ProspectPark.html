Prospect Park

Why did I choose Prospect Park?

I chose this park, because it is one of my favorite parks in the whole of NYC thus far. The other being Greenwood Cemetery. I love this park, because it is the closest thing to a rural landscape I have experienced in this city. The trails make me feel like I am not in a city anymore. It reminds me of my time living in Virginia and North Carolina.

Fun Facts

  1. Prospect Park was designed by architect Calvert Vaux and Frederick Law Olmsted (both also designed Central Park). Vaux and Olmsted said Prospect Park was superior to Central Park.
  2. The Park is 526 acres.
  3. Landscape in the park is based on 18th century British designs.
  4. The Park is divided roughly into thirds: the meadow, water, and forest.
  5. The Parks densely forested paths are self-conscious recreations of an Adirondack wilderness.

Prospect Park "Fun Facts" from the book: Brooklyn: A Journey Through the City of Dreams by Judith Stonehill and Francis Morrone.

What Can I do at the Park?

  1. Sleep on the grassy meadows
  2. Walk your pet
  3. Hit the trails on your bike
  4. Race your bike on the asphalt trail that runs around the park
  5. Bring a hammock and relax in the woods or by the lake
  6. Go ice skating in the winter and rollerblading in the summer
  7. Sit on the benches and watch the world go by
  8. Check out Prospect Park Zoo
  9. Horseback ride in the park
  10. Hop in a kayak or take a pedal boat for a spin on 55 acres of water
  11. Play a game of soccer, frisbee, baseball, or cricket
  12. Check out Prospect Park Zoo
